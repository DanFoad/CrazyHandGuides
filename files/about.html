FAQ

1. There are plenty of guides in Smashboards, why should I go here instead?

- Smashboards is an amazing community. But I intended /r/crazyhand to be place for beginners and pros alike to come together and discuss about the competitive scene of Smash Bros. Smashboards can be a bit intimidating for beginning players wanting to get to the competitive side of Smash Bros. Here, we try our best to keep it straight forward and simple.

2. Some guides on the wiki are not here...

- Yes, there are two reasons why a guide isn't on the site. One is because the guide has not yet been made yet and second because the guide was a 'comment guide' and not a thread guide. Thread guides provide more information than comment guides, so it is preferred that I show those instead.

3. There are ton of other guides on different websites, why not just take those?

Although true, we want these guides to be made by fellow users of the subreddit /r/crazyhand.

4. I want to help!

-Awesome! If you know HTML/CSS/Javascript be sure to head over to GitHub were the files are being held and hosted. If you want certain links to be displayed then tell me ! If you want to make a guide, head over to the subreddit! We will gladly accept your work
